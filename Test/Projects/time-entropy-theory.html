Time-Entropy Interaction Theory (TEIT)

“Time is not merely a passive, linear backdrop to physical events.”

In TEIT, time is understood as a directional gradient — an active agent, channeled through entropy, that interacts with matter and space to create observable effects such as gravity, heat, and quantum oscillation.

Why TEIT?

TEIT offers a potential explanation for phenomena current physics doesn't fully resolve — such as the source of gravitational fields, the link between entropy and time dilation, and the active role of matter in shaping the flow of time.

Experimental Path Forward

By developing a laser containment system and eventually testing entropy-based effects on gravitational sensors in space, we may uncover relationships between temperature, mass, time gradients, and localized spacetime warping.
